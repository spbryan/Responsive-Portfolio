About Me

My career in IT started on a less traditional path than most. I graduated with a degree in History followed by a Master’s degree in Education. The job market for History teachers was pretty thin at the time but the budding “Dot-Com Boom” and looming “Y2K Disaster” presented a new opportunity. Liberty Mutual was willing to take a chance with people from non-technical majors to be trained internally and so I was able to get my foot in the door.

I started my career enhancing a PL1 mainframe system in anticipation of Y2K. By the time the new millenium arrived, and both world and company were still standing, I had shown some aptitude for the profession. I was assigned to a group to design and develop a new Policy Admin system using “cutting edge” technology such as C++ and Aion. Over the years I grew into a technical leadership role and ultimately owner of the domain. Shortly after promotion to Technologist I was asked to refactor this system, this time into web services utilizing Java, BPM and ODM. Years later it is time for another refactor and I am currently in the process of working through designs to break down the monolith, establish distributed microservices, and move to the cloud.

Through it all my family has built a very happy life in coastal New Hampshire. My wife and I have three kids, all now (mostly) grown. We now enjoy a quiet house shared with a feisty Jack Russell , a slightly touched Rat Terrier, and a chinchlla named "Gunther".
